## Mathematical Validation

Complete equation-comparison artifacts were generated by [equation_analysis.py](/content/research-thesis-overthinking-boundary/research/equation_analysis.py) and written to [equation_analysis_report.md](/content/research-thesis-overthinking-boundary/research/reports/equation_analysis_report.md).

Key findings:

- Best overall stop rule: `direct_drift_ridge_top4`
  - LOFO mean test AUC: `0.5890`
  - Boundary accuracy within `±1` step: `0.5358`
  - Mean oracle gap: `0.3216`
- Best hazard-preserving equation: `hazard_quadratic_combo_entropymean_entropystd_confidence_thoughttokencount`
  - LOFO mean test AUC: `0.6151`
  - Boundary accuracy within `±1` step: `0.5111`
  - Mean oracle gap: `0.2512`
- Current baseline `hazard_quadratic_top4`
  - LOFO mean test AUC: `0.6479`
  - Boundary accuracy within `±1` step: `0.3986`
  - Mean oracle gap: `0.3287`

Conclusion:

- If the thesis prioritizes pure stopping accuracy, the direct drift formulation is currently the strongest empirical candidate.
- If the thesis must preserve the original one-crossing hazard interpretation, the current `quadratic_top4` equation should be replaced by the entropy-and-confidence-heavy quadratic variant above.

Recommended hazard-form equation:

$$
\hat{\mu}_t = (1 - \hat{q}_t)\hat{\alpha}_t - \hat{q}_t\hat{\beta}_t - 0.05,
\qquad
\phi(x_t) = \text{quadratic}(\text{entropy\_mean}, \text{entropy\_std}, \text{confidence}, \text{thought\_token\_count})
$$

Coefficient tables for the recommended hazard equation are stored in [recommended_equation_weights.csv](/content/research-thesis-overthinking-boundary/research/outputs/equation_analysis/recommended_equation_weights.csv).

## Observable Rankings

From the completed equation sweep:

- `entropy_std` and `confidence` materially improve hazard-boundary performance when paired with `entropy_mean` and `thought_token_count`.
- `answer_changed` is not required in the best hazard-form equation.
- `hidden_l2_shift` is not required in the best hazard-form equation, even though it remains the strongest hidden-geometry scalar among the tested hidden observables.
- Hidden-geometry ranking on the legacy benchmark is:
  - `hidden_l2_shift`: mean absolute correlation `0.0844`, mean AUC `0.5671`
  - `pca_velocity_norm`: mean absolute correlation `0.0592`, mean AUC `0.5470`
  - `hidden_kl_divergence`: mean absolute correlation `0.0394`, mean AUC `0.4461`

Interpretation:

- Hidden-state geometry still carries signal, but the strongest hazard-form frontier-agnostic signature appears to be entropy concentration plus response-length behavior, not KL drift between pooled hidden states.

## Thesis Implications

- The equation work strengthens the thesis claim that overthinking can be detected from model-agnostic observables.
- The original four-feature quadratic hazard form is not yet optimal.
- The strongest hazard-form replacement shifts emphasis toward entropy variance and confidence rather than answer-change or hidden-state L2 shift.
- The strongest empirical stop rule may be a direct drift estimator rather than a decomposed hazard model, which creates a meaningful thesis choice between structural interpretability and raw stopping accuracy.

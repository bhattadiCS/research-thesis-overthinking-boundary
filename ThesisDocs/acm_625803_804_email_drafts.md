# Email Drafts and Recommended Sequence for 625.803-804 Approval

## Recommended Sequence

Do not start with one joint email to Dr. Pemy and Dr. Castello.

Send Dr. Pemy a direct email first asking him to serve as second reader. You need his explicit agreement before the approval packet is truly complete, and the ACM guidance says the proposal must identify the second reader. Once he agrees, send the full packet to Dr. Castello for academic-adviser approval, with Dr. Woods and Dr. Pemy copied if helpful.

Because your deadline is Friday, the right approach is:

1. Send the email to Dr. Pemy now, with the proposal draft attached.
2. If he agrees, send the approval request to Dr. Castello immediately afterward the same day.
3. If he does not answer quickly, send one short follow-up later today or tomorrow morning.
4. If timing becomes risky, ask Dr. Woods whether he recommends an alternate second reader as a backup.

The main reason not to begin with a single all-in-one email is that Dr. Castello is not the person who decides whether Dr. Pemy will serve. It is cleaner and faster to secure the second reader first, then send the academic-adviser request with the names, proposal, and form aligned.
